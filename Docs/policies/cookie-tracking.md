- What categories of cookies may be used
- How users can manage cookie choices
- How tracking relates to analytics, security, personalization, payments, and advertising
- How this policy works with our Privacy Policy and Data Policy

This policy should be read together with our [Privacy Policy](./privacy-policy.md), [Data Policy](./data-policy.md), [Security Policy](./security-policy.md), and [Terms of Use](./terms-of-use.md).

## 3. What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files placed on a browser or device by a website or service.

Cookies may help a service:

- Keep users signed in
- Remember preferences
- Secure sessions
- Prevent fraud
- Measure performance
- Understand product usage
- Support billing and checkout
- Improve reliability
- Personalize user experience
- Provide analytics
- Support marketing or attribution, where enabled

Cookies may be set by Aerealith AI directly or by third-party providers that help operate Aerealith AI.

## 5. First-Party and Third-Party Cookies

Aerealith AI may use both first-party and third-party cookies.

**First-party cookies** are set by Aerealith AI or domains controlled by SinLess Games LLC.

**Third-party cookies** are set by service providers or third-party platforms used by Aerealith AI, such as hosting, analytics, authentication, payments, support, monitoring, marketing, or embedded content providers.

Third-party providers may process data according to their own terms, privacy policies, and cookie policies.

## 6. Cookie Categories

Aerealith AI may use the following categories of cookies and tracking technologies.

### 6.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

Strictly necessary cookies are required for Aerealith AI to function.

These may be used for:

- Login sessions
- Authentication
- Account security
- Fraud prevention
- Load balancing
- Consent preference storage
- Checkout security
- Access control
- CSRF protection
- Rate limiting
- Workspace routing
- Organization access controls
- Service reliability

These cookies cannot usually be disabled through Aerealith AI cookie settings because the service may not work properly without them.

Users may block these cookies through browser settings, but some features may stop working.

### 6.2 Security Cookies

Security cookies and related technologies help protect users, organizations, and Aerealith AI.

These may be used for:

- Detecting suspicious activity
- Preventing account takeover
- Protecting sessions
- Identifying abuse
- Enforcing rate limits
- Preventing spam
- Protecting APIs
- Supporting audit logs
- Detecting unauthorized access
- Supporting incident response

Security cookies may be considered strictly necessary when they are required to provide a safe and secure service.

### 6.3 Preference Cookies

Preference cookies remember user choices.

These may be used for:

- Theme preference
- Language preference
- Region preference
- Accessibility preferences
- Cookie consent choices
- Interface layout
- Assistant display preferences
- Dashboard preferences
- Documentation preferences

Preference cookies may improve user experience but are not always required for basic functionality.

### 6.4 Analytics and Performance Cookies

Analytics and performance cookies help us understand how Aerealith AI is used and how the service performs.

These may be used to measure:

- Page views
- Feature usage
- Session duration
- Navigation paths
- Referring pages
- Error rates
- Performance metrics
- Load times
- Conversion events
- Documentation usage
- Product adoption
- Aggregate usage trends

Analytics may be used to improve reliability, usability, documentation, onboarding, product design, and support.

Where required, analytics cookies will only be used after consent.

### 6.5 Functional Cookies

Functional cookies support enhanced features.

These may be used for:

- Remembering workspace state
- Saving UI preferences
- Supporting chat continuity
- Remembering recently used projects
- Supporting dashboards
- Supporting marketplace browsing
- Supporting support chat
- Supporting embedded media
- Supporting app-to-docs transitions

Disabling functional cookies may reduce product quality or disable some features.

### 6.6 Personalization Cookies

Personalization cookies and related technologies may help customize the Aerealith AI experience.

These may be used for:

- Remembering product preferences
- Recommending documentation
- Customizing onboarding
- Improving assistant experience
- Adjusting interface behavior
- Remembering selected persona or assistant identity settings
- Showing relevant product guidance
- Supporting workspace-specific experiences

Personalization cookies are different from long-term AI memory, although both may affect user experience.

Memory behavior is governed by the [Privacy Policy](./privacy-policy.md), [Data Policy](./data-policy.md), and user or organization memory settings.

### 6.7 Advertising and Marketing Cookies

Aerealith AI may use advertising or marketing cookies where enabled.

These may be used for:

- Measuring campaign performance
- Understanding referrals
- Limiting repeated ads
- Showing relevant ads
- Measuring signups from ads
- Affiliate or partner attribution
- Retargeting, where permitted
- Preventing advertising fraud

Where required, advertising and marketing cookies will only be used after consent.

Users may opt out of sale, sharing, targeted advertising, or similar activity where applicable.

### 6.8 Payment and Checkout Cookies

Payment processors may use cookies and similar technologies to support payment, fraud prevention, checkout security, tax calculation, and billing workflows.

These may be used by providers such as Stripe, PayPal, or other payment processors made available by SinLess Games LLC.

Payment cookies may be required to complete checkout, detect fraud, process transactions, and comply with payment processor rules.

Embedded content may include:

## 10. No Dark Patterns

Aerealith AI should not use deceptive or manipulative cookie consent designs.

Cookie choice interfaces should be designed to be clear, fair, and understandable.

Where practical, Aerealith AI should avoid:

- Hiding rejection options
- Making rejection unnecessarily difficult
- Using confusing wording
- Using misleading button colors or hierarchy
- Repeatedly asking after a user rejects optional cookies
- Bundling unrelated consent choices
- Claiming optional cookies are required when they are not
- Pre-selecting optional categories where prohibited
- Making consent withdrawal harder than consent acceptance
