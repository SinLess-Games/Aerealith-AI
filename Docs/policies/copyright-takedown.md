# Copyright Takedown Policy

## 1. Purpose

This Copyright Takedown Policy explains how SinLess Games LLC handles copyright complaints, takedown requests, counter-notices, repeat infringers, and copyright-related disputes involving Helix AI.

Helix AI may allow users, organizations, developers, marketplace publishers, and third-party integrations to upload, generate, store, publish, share, transform, summarize, distribute, or otherwise process content. Because that content may include copyrighted material, this policy describes how copyright owners may report alleged infringement and how affected users may respond.

This policy is intended to support lawful copyright enforcement, fair dispute handling, and responsible platform operation.

## 6. Prohibited Copyright Activity

You may not use Helix AI to:

- Upload, publish, share, or distribute copyrighted material without authorization
- Copy, reproduce, or distribute protected works without permission or legal basis

- Misrepresent ownership or licensing of content
- Use Helix AI to mass-copy, scrape, or redistribute copyrighted material in violation of law, contract, or platform rules
- Repeatedly upload or publish infringing content after notice

## 8. Copyright Takedown Notice Requirements

If you believe content available through Helix AI infringes your copyright, you or your authorized agent may submit a written takedown notice to our designated agent.

Your notice should include:

1. A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or a person authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.
2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed.
3. If multiple copyrighted works are covered by one notice, a representative list of those works.
4. Identification of the allegedly infringing material or activity.
5. Information reasonably sufficient to allow us to locate the allegedly infringing material or activity.
6. Your contact information, including name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.
7. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, the copyright owner’s agent, or law.
8. A statement that the information in the notice is accurate.
9. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you are the copyright owner or are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

Incomplete notices may delay processing.

## 9. Helpful Information to Include

To help us process a copyright complaint, include as much detail as possible.

Helpful information may include:

- The URL or exact location of the allegedly infringing content
- The account, workspace, marketplace item, plugin, file, automation, or integration involved
- Screenshots
- File names
- Message IDs
- Marketplace listing IDs
- API object IDs
- Timestamps
- A description of the original copyrighted work
- Registration numbers, if available
- Links to authorized copies of the copyrighted work
- Explanation of how the content allegedly infringes
- Proof that you are authorized to act for the copyright owner, if submitting as an agent

Do not include unnecessary sensitive information.

## 11. Processing Takedown Notices

When we receive a copyright takedown notice, we may:

- Review the notice for completeness
- Request additional information
- Remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material
- Notify the affected user, account owner, workspace administrator, or marketplace publisher
- Preserve records related to the complaint
- Forward the notice to the affected user or organization
- Disable marketplace items, plugins, workflows, templates, or integrations
- Restrict accounts, workspaces, API keys, automations, or publishing features
- Apply repeat-infringer procedures
- Decline to act if the notice is incomplete, invalid, abusive, fraudulent, or unrelated to copyright

We may act quickly when required or appropriate, but submission of a notice does not guarantee removal.

## 12. User Notice After Removal

If we remove or disable access to content because of a copyright takedown notice, we may notify the affected user or organization.

The notice may include:

- The content affected
- The reason for removal
- A copy or summary of the takedown notice
- The complaining party’s contact information
- Instructions for submitting a counter-notice, where available
- Any account, workspace, marketplace, or feature restrictions applied

We may withhold some information if required by law, policy, safety, privacy, security, or abuse-prevention needs.

## 13. Counter-Notice

If your content was removed or disabled because of a copyright takedown notice and you believe the removal was mistaken or the material was misidentified, you may submit a written counter-notice.

Your counter-notice should include:

1. Your physical or electronic signature.
2. Identification of the material that was removed or disabled.
3. The location where the material appeared before it was removed or disabled.
4. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good-faith belief that the material was removed or disabled because of mistake or misidentification.
5. Your name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.
6. A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for the judicial district where your address is located.
7. If your address is outside the United States, a statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of any judicial district where SinLess Games LLC may be found.
8. A statement that you will accept service of process from the person who submitted the original takedown notice or that person’s agent.

Counter-notices should be sent to:

`copyright@sinlessgames.com`

## 14. Counter-Notice Processing

When we receive a valid counter-notice, we may forward it to the party that submitted the original takedown notice.

We may restore the removed or disabled material after the legally required waiting period unless the original complaining party notifies us that they have filed a court action seeking to restrain the user from engaging in the allegedly infringing activity.

We may decline to restore content if:

- The counter-notice is incomplete
- The counter-notice appears fraudulent or abusive
- The content violates another Helix AI policy
- The content creates security, privacy, safety, or legal risk
- The user’s account has been suspended or terminated for other reasons
- Restoration is not technically possible
- A court order, legal obligation, or written agreement prevents restoration

## 15. Repeat Infringer Policy

SinLess Games LLC may terminate or restrict accounts, users, organizations, workspaces, marketplace publishers, plugins, integrations, or developer access for repeat copyright infringement.

Repeat infringement may include:

- Multiple valid copyright takedown notices
- Repeated uploads of the same or similar infringing material
- Repeated publication of infringing marketplace items
- Repeated attempts to evade takedown enforcement
- Repeated use of alternate accounts to publish infringing content
- Repeated copyright abuse through APIs, automations, plugins, or integrations
- Repeated failure to address copyright complaints after notice

Enforcement may include:

- Warning the user or organization
- Removing or disabling content
- Restricting uploads
- Restricting sharing or publishing
- Restricting marketplace access
- Disabling plugins or integrations
- Suspending API keys
- Suspending accounts or workspaces
- Terminating accounts or workspaces
- Refusing future service

The exact action may depend on the severity, frequency, context, validity of notices, user response, and legal obligations.

## 16. Standard Technical Measures

SinLess Games LLC respects standard technical measures used by copyright owners to identify or protect copyrighted works when such measures meet applicable legal requirements.

Users may not interfere with or circumvent standard technical measures, rights-management systems, license checks, provenance systems, watermarking systems, content protection mechanisms, or other lawful copyright-protection controls.

## 17. False, Fraudulent, or Abusive Notices

Do not submit false, misleading, fraudulent, abusive, or bad-faith takedown notices or counter-notices.

Misrepresenting that content or activity is infringing, or that content was removed by mistake or misidentification, may result in legal liability.

We may reject, limit, or take action against notices that appear to be:

- Fraudulent
- Abusive
- Harassing
- Retaliatory
- Automated without reasonable review
- Unsupported by copyright ownership or authorization
- Designed to suppress lawful speech
- Designed to interfere with competitors
- Designed to evade Helix AI policies
- Repeatedly incomplete or inaccurate

We may suspend or terminate accounts that abuse copyright reporting systems.
