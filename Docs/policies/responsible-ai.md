# Responsible AI Policy

## 1. Purpose

This Responsible AI Policy explains how SinLess Games LLC approaches the responsible design, development, deployment, monitoring, and use of artificial intelligence in Helix AI.

Helix AI is designed to help users think, create, automate, analyze, monitor, and act across connected systems. Because AI can affect decisions, workflows, content, data, infrastructure, organizations, and people, Helix AI must be built and operated with clear safeguards, user control, accountability, transparency, and human oversight.

This policy explains:

- Responsible AI principles
- AI risk management
- Human oversight requirements
- Model and tool safeguards
- Automation safeguards
- Data and privacy protections
- Bias and fairness controls
- Testing and evaluation practices
- Red-team and safety review expectations

- Organization and enterprise controls

## 3. Core Responsible AI Principles

Helix AI should be developed and operated according to these principles:

1. **User agency**  
   Users should remain in control of important decisions, connected tools, automations, memory, integrations, and data.

2. **Transparency**  
   Users should understand when they are interacting with AI, what AI may do, what its limits are, and when verification is required.

3. **Human oversight**  
   High-impact, sensitive, regulated, destructive, or irreversible actions should require appropriate human review.

4. **Safety**  
   Helix AI should reduce the risk of harm to users, organizations, systems, data, and the public.

5. **Privacy and data protection**  
   Helix AI should process data only for legitimate service business logic and approved research purposes.

6. **Security**  
   AI features should be designed to resist abuse, unauthorized access, prompt injection, unsafe tool use, and data leakage.

7. **Fairness and bias awareness**  
   Helix AI should be evaluated for harmful bias and should not be used as the sole basis for high-impact decisions about people.

8. **Accountability**  
   AI behavior, tool use, model routing, and automation actions should be logged, reviewable, and governable where practical.

9. **Reliability**  
   AI outputs should be tested, monitored, and improved for accuracy, consistency, and usefulness.

10. **Responsible extensibility**  
   Plugins, marketplace items, developer tools, and automations should follow clear safety, permission, and review requirements.

## 5. No Claim of Sentience or Human Status

Helix AI may use assistant identities, memory, personalization, voices, avatars, personas, and natural language interaction.

However:

- Helix AI is not human.
- Helix AI is not sentient.
- Helix AI does not have consciousness, emotions, independent legal personhood, or personal rights.
- Helix AI may model empathy behaviorally, but it does not experience feelings.
- Helix AI does not replace human judgment, professional responsibility, or user accountability.

Any assistant identity, continuity, personality, or companionship feature is a product feature and should not be interpreted as proof of sentience or human status.

## 11. Accuracy and Hallucination Controls

Helix AI should reduce hallucination risk through reasonable safeguards.

Safeguards may include:

- Source retrieval
- Citations
- Tool verification
- Confidence-aware responses
- Warning users when information may be outdated
- Encouraging verification for high-impact topics
- Limiting claims when evidence is insufficient
- Asking for clarification when necessary
- Distinguishing facts from assumptions
- Using structured outputs for operational tasks
- Testing model behavior
- Monitoring user feedback
- Improving prompts, policies, and retrieval logic

Helix AI should not present uncertain information as guaranteed fact.

- Citations

- Integration names

- User-provided context notes
- Organization-approved source labels

Citations and provenance help users verify output but do not guarantee correctness, completeness, or suitability.

## 13. Data Protection and Responsible AI

Helix AI must respect user data rights and data boundaries.

Responsible AI data practices include:

- Users own their data.
- Helix AI does not sell user data.
- Helix AI does not use user data outside legitimate service business logic and approved research purposes.
- Private Customer Data should not be used to train public or general-purpose models unless clearly disclosed, enabled by settings, or permitted by written agreement.
- Sensitive data should be minimized and protected.
- Secrets should not be stored in ordinary prompts, files, memory, logs, or general user content.
- Access to user data should be limited by role, need, and authorization.
- Research use should prefer aggregated, anonymized, or de-identified data where practical.

Data handling is governed by the [Data Policy](./data.md) and [Privacy Policy](./privacy.md).
